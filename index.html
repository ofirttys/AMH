What is AMH

Anti-Müllerian Hormone (AMH) is a hormone produced by the granulosa cells in ovarian follicles. It is considered a biomarker of ovarian reserve, which reflects the number of eggs remaining in a woman's ovaries.

What AMH Predicts

AMH can help predict:

  • Ovarian reserve and potential reproductive lifespan
  • Response to ovarian stimulation during fertility treatments
  • The likely number of eggs that might be retrieved during IVF
  • The onset of menopause (though with limited accuracy)

What AMH Does Not Predict

AMH cannot predict:

  • Egg quality or chromosomal normality
  • Ability to conceive naturally
  • IVF success rates with certainty
  • Pregnancy outcomes
  • Time to pregnancy
